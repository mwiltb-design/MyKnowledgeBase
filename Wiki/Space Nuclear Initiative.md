## Summary
- **Presidential Mandate**: Established by **NSTM-3** (April 14, 2026), aiming for space nuclear superiority.
- **2030 Launch Target**: A mid-power fission surface reactor ready for deployment on the Moon.
- **Multi-Agency Coordination**: Led by OSTP, involving NASA, DOW (Department of War), and the DOE to synchronize development.

## Strategic Objectives
The initiative focuses on three tiers of nuclear power to unlock exploration, commerce, and defense:
- **Low-power (<1 kWe)**: Cost-effective reactors for near-term exploration.
- **Mid-power (20 kWe+)**: Targeted for 2030 launch; must support at least 3 years of lunar operation.
- **High-power (100 kWe+)**: Extensible designs for the 2030s to support large-scale industrialization.

## Key Directives
- **NTP (Nuclear Thermal Propulsion)**: Focused on high-temperature fuels and radiation-hardened electronics for future Mars missions.
- **NEP (Nuclear Electric Propulsion)**: Prioritizing commonality with surface power systems to accelerate mission readiness by 2031.
- **Commercial Integration**: Heavy use of fixed-price contracts and private-sector innovators to meet aggressive timelines.
