## Summary
- **SR-1 Freedom**: A nuclear electric propulsion (NEP) demonstration repurposing the Power and Propulsion Element (PPE) from the Gateway station.
- **Sustained Power**: Transitioning to nuclear as the primary power source to survive the **120+ hour** lunar night.
- **Capability Targets**: 
  - **Early Phase 1**: Demonstration of **5 kW** power generation (FSP).
  - **Mid-power (2030)**: Mandated **20 kWe** minimum target for launch-ready lunar surface fission.
  - **High-power (2030s)**: Extensible designs reaching **100 kWe+** for industrial-scale operations.

## Nuclear Strategy
The transition to nuclear power is driven by the **[[Space Nuclear Initiative]]** (NSTM-3):
- **NEP (Nuclear Electric Propulsion)**: Targeted for a **2031** mission-enabling demonstration.
- **Surface Power**: Deploying systems capable of generating, storing, and distributing electricity to habitations and mobility assets in shadowed regions.
- **Thermal Management**: Utilizing radioisotope thermal generators (RTGs) to provide night-survival capabilities for systems that can operate in extreme cold.

## Knowledge Challenges
- **Securing Sites**: Identifying and landing at sites with high-resolution mapping of resource locations.
- **Electrical Connections**: Developing dust-tolerant cables and connectors for reliable power sharing across the lunar surface.
