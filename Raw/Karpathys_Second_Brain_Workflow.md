Building a personal knowledge base doesn't require complex software like Obsidian. This workflow uses a simple, AI-driven structure to manage information efficiently.

## The Problem
Most people have a "gold mine of unused knowledge" in bookmarks, notes, and screenshots that never get revisited or organized.

## The Setup (Takes 5 Minutes)
Create three folders on your computer:
1. **Raw:** Where you dump everything (notes, links, screenshots, PDFs).
2. **Wiki:** Where the organized, AI-processed knowledge lives.
3. **Archive:** Where processed raw files are moved for storage.

## The Controller: `Agent.md` or `Commands.md`
A single file that explains the routine to your AI agent. It should contain instructions for syncing, processing, and cleaning up files.

## The Workflow
1. **Fill Your Raw Folder (10 Minutes):** Dump content without worrying about names or organization.
2. **Tell the AI to Build Your Wiki:** Tell the AI to scan the Raw folder, extract data, and format it into clean Markdown files in the Wiki.
3. **The Compounding Loop:** As your Wiki grows (10+ articles), the AI can help you identify gaps in your knowledge or summarize topics based on your own data.
4. **Monthly Health Check (Non-Negotiable):** Review the entire wiki directory. Flag contradictory info and ensure markdown structure remains clean.

## Philosophy: You Don't Need Obsidian
Stop shopping for the perfect tool. Build the system yourself using simple files and folders. Let the AI do the heavy lifting of organizing and extracting value from your data.
